# Gray Logic Go-to-Market Strategy

## Overview

This document outlines the phased approach to building the Gray Logic business from initial proof-of-concept through to a sustainable operation with multiple revenue streams.

**Core principle:** Crawl, walk, run. Prove the product before scaling the business.

## Phase 1: Foundation (Years 1-2)

### Objectives

1. **Prove the product works** in real-world conditions
2. **Refine processes** for installation and support
3. **Build portfolio** of reference projects
4. **Establish credibility** through successful deployments

### Year 1 Focus: Own Home

**Goal:** Complete Gray Logic installation in own home

**Why own home first:**
- No customer pressure or timeline
- Freedom to experiment and learn
- Living with system reveals real issues
- Perfect reference/demo site
- Deep understanding of daily operation

**Target scope:**
- Lighting control (all rooms)
- Climate integration
- Basic presence detection
- Scene management
- Mobile app control

**Success criteria:**
- System runs reliably for 6+ months
- WAF (Wife Acceptance Factor) positive
- Issues identified and resolved
- Confident explaining and demonstrating system

---

### Year 2 Focus: Pilot Projects

**Goal:** Complete 2-3 paid installations

**Target customers:**
- Friends, family, or close contacts
- Patient, understanding clients
- Properties with realistic scope
- Willing to provide testimonials

**Pricing approach:**
- Discounted rates (cost + modest margin)
- Clear communication: "You're a pilot customer"
- Expectation setting: learning process for both parties
- Commitment: full support regardless

**Selection criteria:**
| Factor | Requirement |
|--------|-------------|
| Relationship | Known personally or strong referral |
| Patience | Understanding of development stage |
| Property | Suitable for scope (not too complex) |
| Location | Reasonable travel distance |
| Communication | Responsive, reasonable expectations |

**Deliverables per project:**
- Complete installation to standard process
- Full documentation package
- Professional photography
- Written testimonial
- Video testimonial (if willing)
- Case study written up

---

### Process Refinement

During Phase 1, refine and document:

| Process | What to Learn |
|---------|---------------|
| Site survey | What to check, how long it takes |
| Quotation | How accurate are estimates? |
| Hardware ordering | Lead times, suppliers, reliability |
| Installation | Time per task, sequence, dependencies |
| Configuration | Templates, common patterns |
| Commissioning | Test procedures, common issues |
| Handover | What customers need to know |
| Support | Common questions, issue resolution |

**Documentation produced:**
- Site survey checklist (refined)
- Installation procedures
- Configuration templates
- Commissioning checklist
- Handover pack (finalised)
- Support procedures

## Phase 2: Growth (Years 3-4)

### Objectives

1. **Establish paying customer base** with sustainable flow
2. **Build marketing presence** and brand awareness
3. **Develop referral network** with trade partners
4. **Pilot training programme** for expansion

### Target Volume

| Year | Projects | Average Value | Revenue |
|------|----------|---------------|---------|
| Year 3 | 3-4 | £18,000 | £55,000 - £75,000 |
| Year 4 | 4-6 | £20,000 | £80,000 - £120,000 |

*Assumes part-time operation alongside other work*

---

### Lead Generation Strategy

#### 1. Referral Network (Primary)

**Trade Partners:**
- Architects (residential focus)
- Interior designers
- Builders (high-end new build/renovation)
- Electrical contractors
- AV installers

**Approach:**
1. Identify 5-10 target partners in geographic area
2. Introduce Gray Logic proposition
3. Offer technical briefing / CPD session
4. Provide referral materials
5. Maintain regular contact
6. Thank and reward referrals

**Referral incentive:** £500 per successful project (or reciprocal referrals)

---

#### 2. Existing Customer Referrals

**Strategy:**
- Deliver excellent service (best marketing)
- Ask satisfied customers for referrals
- Make it easy (introduction email template)
- Reward referrals (£500 credit/cash)

**Target:** 25% of new projects from customer referrals

---

#### 3. Portfolio Marketing

**Website:**
- Professional, clean design
- Clear value proposition
- Case studies with photos
- Technical credibility (architecture, open source)
- Easy contact mechanism

**Content:**
- Case study per completed project
- Before/after comparisons
- Technical blog posts (SEO)
- FAQ addressing common concerns

**Social/Professional:**
- LinkedIn profile and company page
- Project photos on Houzz/Instagram
- Technical content on relevant forums

---

#### 4. Local Presence

**Networking:**
- Local business groups
- Construction industry events
- Architect/designer networking events

**PR opportunities:**
- Local business press
- Trade publications
- Speaking at local events

---

### Training Programme Pilot

**Year 4 objective:** Run 1-2 pilot training courses

**Purpose:**
- Test curriculum and materials
- Gather feedback
- Refine delivery
- Build case for Phase 3 expansion

**Pilot approach:**
- Recruit 3-4 trusted electricians
- Heavily discounted rate (£500 vs £1,500)
- Extensive feedback gathering
- Iteration between courses

**Curriculum development:**
- Day 1: Gray Logic architecture, KNX fundamentals, installation
- Day 2: Configuration, commissioning, troubleshooting, handover

See [Certification Programme](certification.md) for full curriculum.

## Phase 3: Scale (Year 5+)

### Objectives

1. **Launch certification programme** for wider reach
2. **Establish hardware revenue stream** through wholesale
3. **Achieve geographic expansion** via partner network
4. **Create exit-viable business** if desired

### Revenue Model Evolution

| Stream | Phase 1-2 | Phase 3+ |
|--------|-----------|----------|
| Direct installation | 100% | 50-60% |
| Training/certification | 0% | 15-20% |
| Hardware wholesale | 0% | 15-20% |
| Consultancy | 0% | 10-15% |
| Support contracts | Minimal | 5-10% |

---

### Certification Programme Launch

**Objective:** 10-15 certified installers by end of Year 5

**Target profile:**
- Qualified electricians (17th/18th Edition)
- Interest in technology/automation
- Customer service orientation
- Geographic diversity (avoid overlap)

**Programme structure:**
- 2-day training course (£1,500)
- First installation support (optional, charged)
- Annual certification fee (£500-1,000)
- Ongoing benefits (hardware discount, leads, support)

See [Certification Programme](certification.md) for details.

---

### Hardware Wholesale

**Gray Logic Hub sales to certified installers:**

| Product | Trade Price | RRP | Installer Margin |
|---------|-------------|-----|------------------|
| Hub Standard | £450 | £800 | £270-350 |
| Hub Pro | £700 | £1,200 | £380-500 |
| Hub Enterprise | £1,200 | £2,000 | £500-800 |

**Volume projections:**
- 10 certified installers
- Average 3 projects/year each
- 30 hubs/year @ £550 avg margin = £16,500/year

---

### Geographic Expansion

**Strategy:** Certified installer network provides national coverage

**Phase 3 target regions:**
- Midlands (first expansion from home base)
- North West
- South East
- Scotland (one partner)

**Lead distribution:**
- Enquiries outside our region referred to nearest certified installer
- Referral fee or reciprocal arrangement
- Quality monitoring on all installations

---

### Consultancy Services

**Target clients:**
- Other installers needing design help
- Architects specifying automation
- Complex projects beyond certified installer capability
- Troubleshooting difficult systems

**Day rate:** £600-800

**Projected volume:** 15-20 days/year = £10,000-15,000

---

### Exit Viability

By end of Phase 3, business should be valuable and sellable:

**Value components:**
- Recurring revenue (support contracts, certification fees)
- Customer base (installed systems)
- Partner network (certified installers)
- Brand and reputation
- Documented processes and systems
- Open source ensures customer continuity

**Potential acquirers:**
- Larger electrical contractors
- AV/automation companies
- Private equity (building trades roll-up)
- Individual buyer (lifestyle business)

**Valuation basis:**
- Recurring revenue × 3-5x
- Plus: customer base, partner network, brand value

## Competitive Positioning

### Key Messages

| Audience | Primary Message |
|----------|-----------------|
| Homeowners | "Professional automation you actually own" |
| Architects | "Open standards, complete documentation" |
| Builders | "Reliable partner, no lock-in for your clients" |
| Electricians | "Add automation to your services" |

### Differentiation Points

**vs Crestron/Savant:**
- Fraction of the cost
- No dealer dependency
- Open source transparency
- Same professional quality

**vs Loxone/Control4:**
- Open source (no lock-in)
- True offline operation
- Built by an electrician
- Transparent pricing

**vs Home Assistant:**
- Professional installation
- Reliable, tested system
- Proper support
- No constant tinkering

## Risk Mitigation

### Phase 1 Risks

| Risk | Mitigation |
|------|------------|
| Product not ready | Own home first, no customer pressure |
| No pilot customers | Friends/family network, discounted rates |
| Technical issues | Time to resolve before paying customers |

### Phase 2 Risks

| Risk | Mitigation |
|------|------------|
| Insufficient leads | Multiple channels, trade partners |
| Pricing wrong | Monitor win rate, adjust |
| Quality issues | Thorough processes, feedback loops |

### Phase 3 Risks

| Risk | Mitigation |
|------|------------|
| Partner quality | Certification standards, monitoring |
| Competition | Differentiation, relationship focus |
| Over-extension | Phased growth, sustainable pace |
